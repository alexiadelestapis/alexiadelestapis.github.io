Implantable Device · FEA · Surgeon Collaboration

Minimally Invasive Intramedullary Fracture Device

Laboratory of Biomechanical Orthopedics, EPFL · In collaboration with Dr. Udin, CHUV Hospital · 2025

2025

Femoral shaft fractures in elderly osteoporotic patients are typically treated with external nailing requiring 10–14 days of immobilization — highly detrimental for weakened bone. Working directly with a CHUV surgeon, I designed a dual-conformation intramedullary device that deploys radially expandable Ti-6Al-4V flanges from inside the bone via a cannulated screw system compatible with existing Stryker surgical tools.

The full pipeline ran in SolidWorks (CAD) and Abaqus (nonlinear FEA) across three simulation steps: deployment, transverse loading, removal. Key design problem: previous iterations couldn't be removed after loading — plastic deformation pushed flanges against bone. My hinge joint design solved this: minimum clearance post-loading is 3.77 mm, identical to insertion.

Robotics · Lab Automation · Cancer Research

Robotic Organoid Micromanipulation Platform

MicroBioRobotic Systems Laboratory, EPFL · Prof. Sakar · 2024

2024

In a culture of human prostate cancer organoids, only certain ones develop a migratory phenotype — identifying them requires extracting individual organoids from matrigel for RNAseq. No robotic protocol existed for aspiration/injection in matrigel. I built one.

Developed control software and protocols for a SmarAct 4-DoF piezoelectric robot performing organoid aspiration and injection under an inverted microscope. Computer vision in Python (OpenCV) for detection, path planning in MATLAB. Characterized matrigel pressure profiles from scratch: aspiration requires −140 mbar, distinct from liquid culture.
